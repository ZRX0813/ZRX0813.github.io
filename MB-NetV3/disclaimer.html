This page describes how third-party and derivative data are used in the MB-NetV3 Inventory Map web application. It may be updated when additional layers or services are added.

Vector overlays (GMBA · IFFI)

On-map polygons and points are derivative visualizations: a GMBA Southern Apennines (Italy) boundary subset combined with IFFI regional open-data extracts (points and landslide polygons), geometrically clipped to that extent for context.

They are not the live national IFFI database, an official ISPRA product, or a complete GMBA Mountain Inventory distribution. For authoritative hazard information, licensing, and redistribution terms, use the official portals and the original dataset releases:

  • GMBA (Global Mountain Biodiversity Assessment) — see the applicable dataset license and citation (e.g. EarthEnv / publication DOI) for the inventory version you use.
  • Progetto IFFI (ISPRA) — respect ISPRA and regional open-data terms (e.g. CC BY-SA where stated).

For academic publication or further redistribution of mapped features, cite the underlying GMBA and IFFI releases you relied on, not this web map alone.

Basemap

The basemap uses CARTO Positron raster tiles built from OpenStreetMap data. Attribution appears on the map per OSM and CARTO requirements.

Place search (Nominatim)

Geocoding uses the public Nominatim service. Use must follow the OpenStreetMap Foundation Nominatim usage policy (fair use, acceptable load, etc.).

Map software

Interactive mapping is provided by Leaflet (BSD-2-Clause).
